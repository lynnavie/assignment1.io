Introduction

Fort Canning Hill is a small hill about 48 metres (156 ft) high in the Southeast portion of Singapore. The 18 hectares park overlooks Orchard Road and is set in the heart of the Civic and Cultural District of Singapore. It is situated near several MRT stations, Fort Canning (DT20), Dhoby Ghuat (NS24/NE6/CC1), and Clarke Quay (NE5).
Visitors can do a variety of recreational activities around the park, such as taking a stroll along its nine historical gardens, have a picnic at Fort Canning Green, or visit its iconic Fort Canning Centre. The park serves as an important green lung for Singapore's downtown city area. The entire place is full of history and culture, waiting for people to uncover its secrets.

Opening Hours:
Fort Canning Park is open to Public 24/7.
Park Lighting Hours: 7.00pm to 7.00am
History of Fort Canning Park
Fort Canning Park is an iconic hilltop landmark that has witnessed many of Singapore's historical milestones.
The hill originally known as Bukit Larangan (or "Forbidden Hill" in Malay) once sited the palaces of 14th Century kings, and also Sir Stamford Raffles' first home.
It was then converted into a fort in 1860 and renamed Fort Canning in honour of Viscount Charles John Canning, Governor-General and first Viceroy of India. In the 1920s and 1930s, the hill was transformed into an artillery fort with barracks, officers' quarters, old gunpowder magazine and a hospital. On 15 February 1942, the decision to surrender Singapore to the Japanese was made on the hill, in the Underground Far East Command Centre, now known as the Battle Box.
Now, Fort Canning Park is a venue for celebrations. Hosting many local and international events on its lawns. With loads of activities for people of all ages, to explore.

Things To Do at Fort Canning Park

Visit The Battle Box




Venture into this historic underground tunnel and discover a labyrinth of rooms and corridors. Each room tells a unique story of the Fall of Singapore.
Watch the war unfold on land and on sea, follow the battles in the air, and discover the tension and drama behind the decision to surrender 'Fortress Singapore' on 15 February 1942.
After a guided tour, visitors can venture into the bunker, unfolding the secrets within.


Operating Hours
The Battlebox is open Fridays to Sundays, and Public Holidays.

Admission Charges
Adult | $20
Child (7-12 Years Old) | $10

Play at Jubilee Park




Located along the western slopes of Fort Canning Park, Jubilee Park is perfect for families, and people of all ages.
Children can enjoy going down the long slides, and hop on the swings and see-saws and climb their hearts out on the logs and balance beams.
Visitors can also look for the Forbidden Spring, a small wading pool for tbose willing to explore.

Operating Hours
Jubilee Park is open 24/7.

Look for the Lighthouse at the Peak




Fort Canning Lighthouse is located right at the top of Fort Canning Hill.
It was formerly one of the 13 important lighthouses in the Straits of Malacca, guiding ships safely towards Singapore Harbour
Although the Lighthouse survived the destruction of World War II, it was eventually dismantled on December 1958.
A replica now stands in its' place, alongside The Flagstaff and the Time Ball.

Operating Hours
Open 24/7.
No entry into the Lighthouse.

Go on a Walking Trail




There are many trails around Fort Canning Park, such as the Ancient History Trail and the Colonial History Trail.
Each trail will lead to someplace different, with lots of flora and fauna and historical elements to spot.
Keep your eyes wide open! You might miss out some of the rarer species of plants and insects living amongst the bushes.

Trail Difficulty
Easy to Moderate. Stay hydrated!

Directions to Fort Canning via MRT
 Fort Canning Station:
Come out from Exit B, turn left to Jubilee Park.
Dhoby Ghuat Station:
Come out from Exit B, cross Penang Road, turn left and keep a lookout for the tunnel leading to Fort Canning Park.
Clarke Quay Station:
Come out from Exit E, turn left and walk along Coleman Bridge.
Turn left again at the end of Coleman Bridge and head towards the pedestrian overhead bridge (100m away) along River Valley Road.
Cross the overhead bridge and you will enter Fort Canning Park.
